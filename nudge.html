Nudge

Nudge is a project born from my own struggles with overusing social media apps like Instagram and TikTok.

Personally, I found the native Digital Wellbeing app on Android too passive and, at the same time, too rigid. It would completely block access to an app once the daily limit was reached, but it was also easy to override by simply dismissing the dialog and removing the timer.

I realized I needed a more persistent, real-time form of feedback. Thatâ€™s when I came up with the idea for Nudge, an app that gently, but firmly, "nudges" users through constant haptic feedback when they exceed their set screen time limits.

When a user goes over their limit in Nudge, the phone starts vibrating and a notification appears encouraging them to lock their device. The vibration only stops once the phone is locked, creating a direct and immediate incentive to disconnect.

Current Work

Users set daily limits on applications between 1 and 60 minutes

Users choose which apps to monitor.

When limits are exceeded, the phone vibrates and prompts the user to lock their device.

Future Work

I'm currently working on implementing social features for Nudge. From my experience, developing healthier habits is not only more fun with a friend, but having someone else involved also builds a sense of accountability, which makes it easier to stick to those habits.

With that in mind, I want to give users the option to log in, view their daily app usage, track their most distracting apps, and have a "Nudge Score", all of which can be shared with friends they add on the app.

Users will also be able to send "nudges" to their friends, a gentle way to check in or encourage someone to put their phone down, reinforcing that mutual accountability loop.
